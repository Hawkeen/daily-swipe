Episode of the day · 🎧
WarTalk: The BS Détente
Ely Ratner on Taiwan, allies, and getting China right
ChinaTalk · host Jordan Schneider · 25 June 2026
Swipe →
3 / 10 →
The hook
A “BS détente” with Beijing looks like calm — but it leaves US allies unwilling to stick their necks out on China.
Former Pentagon Indo-Pacific chief Ely Ratner joins Jordan Schneider, Bryan Clark, Tony and Justin to ask what a real China strategy looks like — starting with Taiwan.
4 / 10 →
The core argument
Getting China right means spending where it counts — and convincing a war-weary public.
The conversation ranges across Taiwan’s defence, allied confidence, and the home-front politics of competition — with the just-concluded Iran war hanging over every question of American staying power.
5 / 10 →
Finding 1 / 4 · Taiwan
Is Taiwan the better dollar-for-dollar buy?
One thread: whether backing Taiwan’s own military is a better dollar-for-dollar return than spending on US forces. Ratner ties it to Taiwan’s “resilience agenda” and what he saw on a recent trip to Taipei.
6 / 10 →
Finding 2 / 4 · Politics
Selling competition to a tired electorate
The political puzzle: how do you sell sustained “China competition” to voters who are done with forever wars? Without that domestic buy-in, allies stay wary of leaning in.
7 / 10 →
Finding 3 / 4 · The seam
China plays the seam of US politics
From the spy balloon to the Volt Typhoon intrusions, the panel argues Beijing exploits the seam of US domestic politics — the gap between what Washington says and what it can sustain at home.
8 / 10 →
Finding 4 / 4 · The Iran shadow
What Taiwan learns from Iran
In a war game in Japan, Bryan Clark notes, allies took quiet satisfaction that Iran withstood US strikes — asking whether the “firepower strike complex” China relies on is really up to 21st-century war. The panel also debates consolidating combatant commands and a new drone command, “RoboCom.”
9 / 10 →
Why this matters in 2026
Recorded in the wake of the Iran war ceasefire
The episode (25 June 2026) opens on the new US–Iran MOU. Bryan Clark calls the terms “fairly in Iran’s favor”; Justin argues the side that had to move first — the US lifting the blockade — is usually the side on the receiving end. That fresh test of American resolve shadows the whole Taiwan discussion.
10 / 10 ✓
The takeaway
“The Venn diagram between what Iran is talking about and what the administration is talking about feels like almost zero.”
— Ely Ratner, on the BS Détente
