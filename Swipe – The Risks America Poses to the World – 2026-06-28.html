Episode of the day · 🎧
The Risks America Poses to the World
Ian Bremmer on Iran, the Trump–Xi summit, and a foreign policy losing coherence
The Ezra Klein Show · with Ian Bremmer
The New York Times · 2 June 2026 · 1h 32m

The Hook
“Our foreign policy has entered into a period of absolute incoherence.”
That is how Ezra Klein frames the moment: two huge stories — a war with Iran and a much-anticipated Trump–Xi summit — that, looked at closely, no longer add up to a clear strategy.
4 / 10 →
The Premise
The biggest risk in the world may now be America itself.
Ezra wanted “to do an episode looking at China and Iran and trying to assess Trump’s foreign policy in general and the ways he’s remaking what America means on the world stage.” His guest, political-risk analyst Ian Bremmer, is built for exactly that question.
5 / 10 →
Finding 1 / 5 · Two stories
Iran and the Trump–Xi summit
Over the past month two stories have dominated American foreign policy: the war with Iran, and the much-anticipated summit between President Trump and Xi Jinping of China. The episode uses both as a lens on where US strategy is actually heading.
6 / 10 →
Finding 2 / 5 · The Iran question
“What is Trump trying to achieve?”
Klein is candid that even he is “not even sure what the status of the Iran war is at this point” — what Trump is trying to achieve, or what he is willing to accept. The uncertainty itself is the story.
7 / 10 →
Finding 3 / 5 · The China turn
A consistent principle, suddenly unclear
Taking a hawkish line on China has been a core, consistent Trump principle since his first term — that China took advantage of the US and that America had to flex more power. The open question now: is that still even Trump’s position?
8 / 10 →
Finding 4 / 5 · The analyst
Who is Ian Bremmer?
Bremmer is president and founder of Eurasia Group, a political-risk research and consultancy firm, and of the global-affairs publication GZero. He is the author of, among other books, “Every Nation for Itself: What Happens When No One Leads the World” — a guide to a world with no one in charge.
9 / 10 →
Finding 5 / 5 · The reframing
From global cop to global variable
The episode’s title says the quiet part out loud: this is about the risks America poses to the world. The unsettling shift is that the country long seen as the anchor of the order is now itself a leading source of unpredictability.
