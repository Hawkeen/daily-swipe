Episode of the day · 🎧
Running a Bank in the Age of AI
Odd Lots · Tracy Alloway & Joe Weisenthal · Bloomberg · 4 June 2026
Guest: David Solomon, Chairman & CEO of Goldman Sachs
The machine drafts the paperwork. Who’s left to sign it?
Swipe →
3 / 11 →
The hook
AI can now write 95% of an IPO filing in minutes — work that once took a six-person team two weeks.
It’s the kind of number that should terrify a junior banker. Yet the CEO whose firm sits at the centre of Wall Street says he doesn’t expect a white-collar wipeout. The episode is his case for why.
4 / 11 →
The core argument
AI is a productivity layer that rewires daily tasks — not a one-time round of job cuts.
Solomon’s bet: deployed across back-office staff, junior analysts and senior bankers alike, AI makes smart people more productive and shifts freed-up hours toward higher-value client work. Get the transition right, he argues, and Goldman doesn’t end up employing fewer people.
5 / 11 →
Finding 1 / 5
The 95% number
Solomon has said that AI can complete 95% of an S-1 filing — the core IPO registration document — in just a few minutes, whereas it used to take a six-person team two weeks. It’s his headline example of how far the tooling has already come inside the bank.
6 / 11 →
Finding 2 / 5
A 20% boost for the coders
It isn’t just bankers. Goldman’s own software engineers saw roughly a 20% productivity boost from AI coding-assistant tools. “When you put these tools in the hands of smart people,” Solomon says, “it increases their productivity.”
7 / 11 →
Finding 3 / 5
25% of work hours — redirected, not deleted
Goldman research suggests AI could automate about 25% of work hours. Solomon’s framing: that freed time shifts toward higher-value client work, rewarding people who learn the new tools rather than triggering a one-time cull.
8 / 11 →
Finding 4 / 5
“I don’t think it works that way”
Asked directly whether Goldman will simply employ fewer people, Solomon pushed back: “If you’re… assuming an organization like Goldman Sachs is just going to have less people, I don’t think it works that way.” Job functions change; the headcount, he argues, need not collapse.
9 / 11 →
Finding 5 / 5
Bubble? “Maybe — but it’s not different”
Set against yesterday’s permabear: Solomon has said AI may well be a bubble, but “it’s not different” from earlier market manias. The optimist and the bear can agree the technology is real — and still disagree fiercely about the price.
10 / 11 →
Why this matters in 2026
He said it as the IPO window cracked open
Solomon spoke as Goldman lined up for the upcoming SpaceX IPO and Alphabet’s historic equity capital raise — the very deals where AI now drafts the paperwork. The question of whether productivity gains shrink the workforce isn’t abstract; it’s being decided on live mandates right now.
